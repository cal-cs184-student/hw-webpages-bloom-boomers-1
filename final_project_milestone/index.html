Summary

As we work towards our final goal of not only accurately simulating fluid physics, but also rendering it, we split this project into 2 parts: physics and rendering. Our goal for the milestone was finishing as much as possible the physics of the fluid. To be more precise, that meant implementing fundamental data structures such as the Fluid and the Particles that make up the fluid, representing the particles as simple spheres, and allowing particles to bounce off boundaries and influence nearby particles (repeling close ones, while attracting others that are further away). Although we accomplished that goal, there is still improvements that can be made to the physics of the particles.

Preliminary Results

As of now we are able to simulate fluids with about 1000 particles without siginificant FPS drops. We start seeing FPS drops at around 1500 particles. The particles themselves also bounce off of boundaries, and hold some influence to one another. We are also able to modify the fluid's properties in real time through the GUI, which was initially a target deliverable.

Reflection

Although there is room for improvement, we are quite satisfied with our progress, considering our group had a last minute change of project proposal. Moving forward, we aim to tweak the physics in order to achieve better results, as well as explore optimization options to further improve performance. We are nonetheless on track to delivering on our target goals.
